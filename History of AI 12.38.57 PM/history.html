History of AI

Groundwork of AI

1921

Czech playwright Karel Capek released a science fiction play "Rossum's Universal Robots" which had shown the idea of artificial people. This is the first known use of the word.

1929

Japanese professor Makoto Nishimura built the first japanese robot, which was named Gakutensoku.

1943

Researchers Warren McCulloch and Walter Pitts present their artificial neurons model, which is considered to be the first artificial intelligence.

1949

Computer Scientist Edmund Berkley published the book Giant Brains or machines that think which had compared the newer models of computers to human brains.

The birth of AI

1950

British mathematician Alan Turing proposed a test for machine intelligence. The test was if the machine could fool humans into thinking it is human then it has the intelligence.

1952

A computer scientist named Arthur Samuel developed a program that could play checkers. WHich was the first to learn to play the game independently.

1955

John McCarthy had a workshop on artificial intelligence which is the first use of the word, and how it came into popular usage.

Maturation of AI

1958

John McCarthy created List Processing which was the first programming language for AI research, which is still in popular use today.

1959

Arthur Samuel coined the term Machine Learning when he was doing a speech on teaching machines to play chess better than the people who programmed them.

1961

The first industrial robot Unimate started working on an assembly line at General Motors in New Jersey, it was tasked with transporting die casings and welding parts on cars.

1965

Edward Feigenbaum and Joshua Lederberg created the first expert system which is a form of AI programmed to replicate the thinking and decision making abilities of human experts.

1966

Joseph Weizenbaum created the first chatterbot, an example of this is ELIZA a mock psychotherapist that used natural language processing to converse with humans.

1973

A mathematician named James Lighthill gave a report to the British Science Council, which underlined that strides were not as impressive as those that had been promised by scientists, which led to much reduced support and funding for AI research from the British government.

1979

James L. Adams created the standford cart in the year 1961, which became one of the first examples of an autonomous vehicle. It successfully navigated a room full of chairs without human interference. The American Association of Artificial Intelligence which is now called Association for the Advancement of Artificial Intelligence was founded.
